Painting the spaces where memory, topography, and light intersect.

Process

Intentionally tactile from first sketch to framed piece

Eleanor builds each series through cycles of on-location field notes, layered studio experimentation, and detailed archival finishing by hand.

Fieldwork

Walking the shoreline

Site sketches made with graphite and ink capture tide lines, weather patterns, and fragments of remembered conversations.

Material Research

Transparent layers

Pigments are suspended in cold wax and resin to create translucent veils that glow when light moves across the surface.

Finishing

Custom framing

Each piece is framed in reclaimed oak with museum-grade glazing to protect the subtle depth of the work.

About the artist

Eleanor Strite

Based in Minneapolis, Eleanor creates paintings and mixed media installations inspired by watersheds and memory maps. She has exhibited across the Midwest, Pacific Northwest, and coastal galleries, and her work lives in private collections around the world.

When she is not in the studio, she leads community workshops that help people translate personal histories into visual narratives through color, collage, and experimental mark-making.
